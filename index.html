How do you know you are an addict?

 

Common signs of addiction include:

  1. Changes in social groups, new and unusual friends, odd phone conversations
  2. Drug paraphernalia such as unusual pipes, cigarette papers, small weighing scales, etc.
  3. Stealing
  4. Stashes of drugs, often in small plastic, paper, or foil packages

Common symptoms of addiction include:

  1. Activities centering on the addiction in a way that negatively affects relationships, school, and work
  2. A preoccupation with the addiction and spending a lot of time on planning, engaging in, and recovering from the addictive behavior
  3. Changes in energy, such as being unexpectedly and extremely tired or energetic
  4. Difficulty cutting down or controlling the addictive behavior
  5. Extreme mood changes
  6. Physical changes including increased illness and changes in weight
  7. Sleeping a lot more or less than usual, or at different times of the day or night

Symptoms of Specific Addictions

  • Behaviors (gambling, sex, shopping): Behavioral addictions are characterized by compulsive behaviors that persist despite negative consequences.
  • Depressants (alcohol, barbiturates, benzodiazepines): These medications slow the activity of the central nervous system and can lead to slowed heartbeat and respiration, confusion, coma, and death.
  • Opioids (painkillers, heroin, morphine): These substances decrease sensitivity to pain and produce strong cravings for opioids.
  • Stimulants (caffeine, nicotine, amphetamines, methamphetamine, cocaine): These substances lead to increased energy levels.

 

Why is it important to get treatment?

Addiction can cause:

  • Accidents
  • Family problems
  • Financial problems
  • Health problems
  • Legal issues
  • Relationship problems
  • School difficulties
  • Suicide
  • Work problems
